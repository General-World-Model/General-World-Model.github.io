Overview

We invite researchers, students, and practitioners to contribute talks to our seminar series on General World Models. This series focuses on emerging approaches to modeling how the world evolves over time, integrating perception, geometry, dynamics, and decision-making into unified frameworks. We welcome presentations covering a broad range of topics, including video-based world models, diffusion and flow-based dynamics modeling, geometry-aware representations, multimodal learning, embodied AI, robotics, and long-horizon planning.

The goal of this seminar is to foster interdisciplinary discussion and exchange ideas on building scalable, robust, and generalizable world models that can support intelligent agents operating in complex environments. Both theoretical and applied perspectives are encouraged, as well as early-stage research, system demonstrations, and critical analyses of current limitations.

If you are interested in sharing your work or leading a discussion session, we warmly encourage you to submit a talk proposal. We look forward to engaging conversations and collaborative exploration of this rapidly evolving research area.
