What to submit

We welcome original research, systems, datasets, benchmarks, and position papers that advance multi-sensory embodied intelligence — with a 2026 focus on hand–object dexterity manipulation.

Proceedings
Accepted papers will appear in the CVPR workshops proceedings and must follow CVPR style, format, and length requirements.

Tracks & topics

A main “emerging topics” track plus complementary sub-tracks.

Main track

Main track (Emerging Topics)

Multi-modal sensing algorithms and new hardware.

Sub tracks

  • Human modeling & understanding: face, body, pose, gesture, movement
  • Multimodal learning
  • Robot perception
  • Embodied vision: active agents, simulation
  • Vision applications and systems

Suggested topics

  • Sensing & Data: data-driven sensor design; multi-sensory systems; large-scale multi-sensory data acquisition; spatial modeling.
  • Models & Learning: cross-modal representation learning; multi-sensory simulation; adaptive policies; generative models robust to missing modalities.
  • Tasks & Applications: visual–tactile fusion for manipulation; material & texture modeling; learning from bio-signals; language-anchored reasoning grounded in sensory input.
  • Benchmarks & Datasets: resources for multi-sensory spatial understanding and hand–object interaction.
Ethics & responsibility
We encourage submissions that consider privacy, responsible sensing, and equitable impact in embodied AI.
