Workshop Overview

A general world model aims to learn a unified representation of how the world evolves over time, capturing both physical dynamics and scene structure. Unlike task-specific models that focus on narrow prediction objectives, a general world model seeks to understand multi-modal information—such as RGB, depth, geometry, and actions—and predict future states in a coherent and physically consistent manner. By modeling temporal dynamics and spatial structure jointly, it can simulate future observations, reason about object interactions, and support decision-making in complex environments.

In robotics and embodied AI, general world models are particularly important because they enable long-horizon planning, counterfactual reasoning, and adaptation to novel scenarios. Rather than memorizing behavior patterns, the model learns underlying dynamics, allowing it to generalize across tasks and domains. Recent advances in large-scale video diffusion models, geometry-aware representations, and multimodal learning have significantly improved world modeling capabilities. Ultimately, a general world model serves as a foundation for intelligent agents that can perceive, predict, and act in the real world with robustness and flexibility.
